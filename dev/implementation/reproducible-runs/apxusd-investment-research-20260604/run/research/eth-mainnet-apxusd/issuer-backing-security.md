## 2. Issuer / protocol and business model

### Mechanism

Apyx docs describe apxUSD as a synthetic dollar backed by preferred shares issued by Digital Asset Treasuries (DATs). The docs state that apxUSD is intended as collateral and quote asset across DeFi/CeFi, while apyUSD is the savings asset that accrues rewards from dividends generated by the same collateral stack. Sources: S2/S3 medium.

The public contracts README describes Apyx as a dividend-backed stablecoin protocol transforming DAT preferred equity into programmable digital dollars. It states that apxUSD is the base stablecoin and apyUSD is the yield-bearing wrapper. Source: S8 medium.

### Business / yield source

- apxUSD itself is the base synthetic dollar; yield from the collateral stack is primarily surfaced through apyUSD. Source: S3/S8 medium.
- Preferred-share dividends are the economic source described for the broader protocol and apyUSD yield. Source: S2/S3/S8 medium.
- The collateral basket is described as dynamically allocated across preferred shares issued by various DATs, with rebalancing subject to issuer concentration, liquidity requirements, and coverage requirements. Source: S2 medium.

### Dependencies and controls

- Offchain dependencies: DAT preferred-share issuers, custodians/attestors, collateral valuation, liquidity of preferred shares, and Apyx operational execution around collateral allocation and redemptions. Sources: S2/S4/S5.
- Contract dependencies: apxUSD UUPS implementation, AccessManager `0xe167...2824`, MinterV0 `0x2c36...a76e`, deny-list `0x2c271d...F6AA`, Safe-like role holders, and CCIP/token-admin configuration. Source: S1 high.
- Direct redemption: official docs say eligible participants in permitted jurisdictions who are whitelisted, such as institutional market makers, may mint and redeem apxUSD through designated issuance/redemption pathways; redemptions settle in USDC and holders do not receive preferred shares directly. Source: S2 medium.
- General users: docs say general users can acquire apxUSD through permissionless external liquidity pools and swaps. Source: S2 medium.

## 3. Backing, NAV, and exposure map

`nav_model: issuer NAV / offchain preferred-share collateral / overcollateralized synthetic dollar`

### Backing model

- Official docs: apxUSD is backed by a basket of low-volatility, variable-rate preferred shares issued by DATs. Source: S2 medium.
- Collateral allocation: the basket rebalances subject to issuer concentration, liquidity requirements for efficient execution, and coverage requirements intended to keep apxUSD overcollateralized. Source: S2 medium.
- Redemption scenarios: protocol liquidates preferred shares to USDC to settle redemption obligations; redeeming participants do not receive preferred shares directly. Source: S2 medium.
- Transparency: official Apyx docs reference the Accountable dashboard as independent near-real-time visibility into backing assets, including supply, reserves, and collateral coverage; they also reference an Apyx dashboard and Dune dashboard. Source: S4 medium.
- Attestations: official docs list monthly Wolf & Company attestation opinions for March and April 2026 and state custodians provide monthly attestations validating backing assets exist, remain under custody control, and are valued appropriately. Sources: S4/S5 medium.

### Peg stability and exposure caveats

Apyx docs describe four peg-stability mechanisms:

1. Preferred-share price-stabilization dynamics.
2. Overcollateralized issuance framework.
3. Cross-market arbitrage by Apyx and eligible whitelisted users.
4. Derivative-based tail hedging strategies.

Source: S2 medium.

Exposure caveats:

- Backing is not simple onchain USDC. It depends on offchain preferred-share collateral, issuer credit/dividend dynamics, custody, valuation, and liquidation to USDC in redemption scenarios. Sources: S2/S4/S5.
- The docs state eligible whitelisted participants may redeem; general users relying on DEX liquidity may not have primary redemption access. Source: S2.
- This pass did not independently validate Accountable data, attestation PDF contents, preferred-share issuer quality, collateral concentration, or current coverage ratio against onchain supply. `missing_behavior: review_required`.
